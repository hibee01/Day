02.

The Problem

With competitor services offering a variety of social aspects to music with varying amounts of success, The current music apps in the market does not have any noteworthy social features to offer plus a high monthly subscription fees and no affordable plans for student users. Users share music to friends in a variety of ways which includes messaging apps, screenshots on whatsapp status, word of mouth, notes, etc.

The Goal

Design a music app that allows users listen to their favorite songs, playlists or albums on any platform with a clean and easy to use interface. Integrate social features where users can share their playlist with family & friends via the app. Users will be able to know & listen to their friends playlists in real-time. Affordable subscription plans for all kinds of users

Interviews & Surveys

In order to find a solution I set out to better understand how people relate, share and experience music together in order to find a proper solution within the app that delivers the social experience users are missing. To kickoff the project I conducted user interviews with six participants to gain a better understanding of how people listen to music, if participants shared music with friends and the underlying motivations for sharing and streaming music. To help me gain insight, I did some qualitative research method by conducting user interviews with participants to receive enough data.

  • Participants typically share music through links via direct message, playlists screenshots on their whatsapp status or through social media apps (Instagram stories, dating apps).
  • Sharing music helps connect them both with friends who live in different cities or create bonds with friends they see on a day-to-day basis.
  • Memorable experiences usually involved trips with friends and creating collaborative playlists together.
  • Participants like the curated playlists that has the best algorithm.
  • Affordable monthly subscription fees and ability to share subscription plans with family & friends.

Define

Based on the takeaways from my research, I created the ideal persona, Nina Evans. Her goals, motivations, frustrations and loves reflect the users I interviewed. Nina Evans our hypothetical user created below represents a type of user in the database whom I will be using as a reference point in design decisions going forward.
